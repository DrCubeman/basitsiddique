Basit's Website

General:

This is something I’m really proud of. Using knowledge I had gained from my previous Buy N Large Website, I really wanted to build my own website.

I read in several places that you should have your own website, especially in our field of programming and web design. So I set out to do just that.

During Development:

I usually always plan something with knowledge of my current skill level of what this project is made with, and what themes I like in mind.

Although this was the case here, I still ended up scrapping several early copies of this site, because it didn’t fit me.

What fits me?

Well, I gathered some things and themes, I do like.

  • The colour Black
  • Cyberpunk and Sci-Fi aesthetic
  • Black Minimalism
  • Simplisty

    • I took inspiration from some other personal sites, whereas the homescreen had several buttons in the middle, along with their name.

    Which I liked, nice, simple and minimalist. A personal spin I added was, well if you go to the homepage, those red lines turning white, that go up down,

    well I was actually inspired by a scene in John Wick Chapter 4. (spoilers)

    In the Osaka Continental, there are a lot of these LED blocks that even resemble an archway, and I thought that the lights used around this hotel were very nice,

    and I designed my own version of it, which was a lot more simple, but I ended up really liking it.

    For the entire site, I based it off what the final homepage looked like, black, white, and a bit of colour here and there. Something I felt comfortable with and really spoke to my personality a bit.

    I used a bunch of skills I had learned during the development of the Buy N Large Website, such as Flexbox (most notability on the “About Me” page, where my favorite movies are, borders,

    (like for buttons and also on the about me page, the borders surrounding the titles of the programming languages I know) Animations, such as the those red and white markers, and even played around with the @media feature in CSS,

    where basically, if for example, on the homepage if the page squishes a bit, hide the markers, I did this primarily because of the way I made the markers, if they even used Flexbox,

    they would off center a bit and not show a couple, so I just decided to hide them entirely, because I wanted to have an equal, or none, either 2 and 2 or 3 or 3.

    All-In-All:

    This was more a less taking everything I learned and making a meal with it, and I’m happy with the way my website turned out. I like simple, minimalist black, while having my own little spin on

    it. And I got to learn more about CSS, such as the @media function, but also more strategies,

    like for example setting the font of an entire document using one function rather than individually doing it, if the entire document is using that one font for example.
